Tribute to Steve Jobs


Co-founder of Apple Inc


1955 - 2011

Biography

Steve Jobs, born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, was an American entrepreneur and co-founder of Apple Inc. Known for his visionary leadership and innovative spirit, Jobs played a pivotal role in revolutionizing the technology industry. After dropping out of college, Jobs, along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne, co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. in 1976. The company's mission was to develop and market user-friendly personal computers. Under Jobs' leadership, Apple introduced groundbreaking products such as the Apple II, Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, and iPad, reshaping entire industries and setting new standards for design and innovation.

1955 - 1976: Early Life and Formation of Apple February 24, 1955: Steve Jobs is born in San Francisco, California. 1972: Jobs enrolls at Reed College but drops out after six months. April 1, 1976: Apple Computer, Inc. is founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. 1976 - 1985: Apple's Early Years and Departure 1977: Apple II, the company's first mass-produced personal computer, is released. 1983: Jobs launches the Lisa computer, followed by the Macintosh in 1984. 1985: Jobs is ousted from Apple after a power struggle with the board of directors.


1985 - 1996: NeXT and Pixar 1985: Jobs founds NeXT Computer, Inc., a computer platform development company. 1986: Pixar Animation Studios is founded by Jobs after purchasing the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm. 1995: Pixar releases "Toy Story," the world's first feature-length computer-animated film. 1997 - 2011: Return to Apple and Later Years 1997: Apple acquires NeXT, bringing Jobs back to the company. 1998: Jobs introduces the iMac, marking the beginning of Apple's resurgence. 2001: The iPod is launched, revolutionizing the music industry. 2007: The iPhone is unveiled, transforming the mobile phone industry. 2010: Apple introduces the iPad, popularizing tablet computing. August 24, 2011: Steve Jobs resigns as CEO of Apple due to health issues. October 5, 2011: Steve Jobs passes away at the age of 56.
